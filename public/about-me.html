After that, in college, I competed in Spartahack 8, a hackathon put on by the student coding club at Michigan State where I created a website in 24 hours that took in political articles and used a natural language processor trained on articles that were graded by PolitiFact to pick up on tells of lying. Artificial Intelligence is not to the point yet where it can determine the truth values of certain statements, but it is skilled enough to pick up on tells. The articles were sorted into five categories based on truth value and the model was able to put the articles in the correct place 44% of the time. The articles that were found to not have lying tells would be linked on the front page and article that contained language commonly found in misleading articles and news stories were put on a back page called the graveyard. Our team won the Domain.com best branding award.

I have also been a part of enterprises and extracurricular competitions on those subjects. I was a part of the IT Oxygen Enterprise where I worked on a project in cooperation with Miller Welding to determine the quality of welds created by certain machines. The welding machines could record the current, voltage, wire feed speeds, as well as time spent on a certain weld. Our team created clustering models that could identify which group of welds were for different parts. Then, our model was capable of detecting abnormalities in the data. For example, whether the part had all the welds needed. In those cases, a supervisor would be alerted to assure the quality of that part.
